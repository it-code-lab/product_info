Buying Guide: Choosing the Right Dash Camera

  1. Resolution: Higher resolution ensures clearer footage. Choose 4K if video clarity is a priority.

  2. Night Vision: Look for HDR/WDR or infrared technology for better low-light performance.

  3. Field of View: Wider angles reduce blind spots.

  4. Parking Mode: Continuous surveillance is crucial for parked vehicles.

  5. Storage Options: Ensure the dash cam supports sufficient SD card capacity.

  6. Ease of Installation: Compact and plug-and-play options are user-friendly.
